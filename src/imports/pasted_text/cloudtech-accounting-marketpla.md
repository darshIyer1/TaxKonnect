Now take the completed CloudTech Accounting Practice Marketplace project and elevate it beyond the baseline requirements.

Do not restart, replace the existing concept, change the business model, or add unrelated product ideas.

Your job is to go above and beyond only where it strengthens the product we are already creating:

* A broker-led marketplace for buying and selling accounting and tax practices
* Human-led and system-assisted
* Seller-controlled
* Confidential
* Operationally realistic
* Designed for real transactions
* Presentable to CloudTech leadership

Use strong product judgment, but stay tightly inside this scope.

# 1. PRESERVE THE CORE MODEL

Do not change these principles:

* CloudTech people remain involved at major judgment points
* Sellers control private buyer access
* Anonymous discovery happens before private disclosure
* NDA signing does not release files
* General match recommendations are separate from specific access requests
* Seller approval is separate from CloudTech document release
* Offers require document access
* Diligence requires an accepted offer
* No action may skip required prerequisites

Do not introduce:

* Auctions
* Public bidding
* AI-generated deal decisions
* Automatic seller approval
* Automatic file release
* Social networking
* Public seller identities
* Cryptocurrency
* Gamification
* Unrelated accounting software
* Generic CRM features that do not support the transaction
* Features designed for huge investment-bank transactions rather than accounting-practice sales

# 2. IMPROVE PRODUCT DEPTH

Review every existing screen and ask:

* What decision is the user making here?
* What information do they need?
* What could confuse them?
* What could cause a confidentiality mistake?
* What would a real CloudTech employee need to know?
* What happens immediately before and after this screen?
* Is the current owner and next action obvious?
* Is the human CloudTech involvement visible?
* Are important gates clearly explained?

Improve screens based on those questions.

Do not add content merely to fill space.

Every added element must serve:

* Trust
* Clarity
* Confidentiality
* Transaction control
* Human coordination
* Decision-making
* Reduced user confusion

Make human involvement visible through:

* Review notes
* Preparation guidance
* Human recommendation summaries
* Seller-facing explanations

* Meeting preparation

* Diligence coordination

At every major stage, make it clear:

* What the user does
* What CloudTech does
* What the system records
* What decision comes next

Add contextual human-touchpoint callouts where they improve understanding, but do not repeat the same generic message on every screen.
